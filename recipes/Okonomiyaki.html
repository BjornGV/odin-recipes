Okonomiyaki

A popular street food from Osaka, Okonomiyaki is a delicious Japanese savory pancake “grilled as you like it“
with your choice of protein and tasty condiments and toppings.
This recipe includes the 6 key ingredients that give your Okonomiyaki a truly authentic taste.

Batter Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour (plain flour)
  • ¼ tsp Diamond Crystal kosher salt
  • ¼ tsp sugar
  • ¼ tsp baking powder
  • 5.6 oz nagaimo/yamaimo (mountain yam)
  • ¾ cup dashi (Japanese soup stock)
  • 4 large eggs (50 g each w/o shell)
  • ½ cup tenkasu/agedama (tempura scraps)
  • ¼ cup pickled red ginger (beni shoga or kizami beni shoga)

Other Ingredients

  • 1 head green cabbage
  • ½ lb sliced pork belly
  • neutral-flavored oil

Okonomoyaki Sauce

  • 1½ Tbsp sugar
  • 2 Tbsp oyster sauce
  • ¼ cup ketchup
  • 3½ Tbsp Worcestershire sauce

Steps for the batter

  1. In a large bowl, combine 1 cup all-purpose flour (plain flour),
    ¼ tsp Diamond Crystal kosher salt, ¼ tsp sugar, and ¼ tsp baking powder
    and mix all together.
  2. Peel and grate 5.6 oz nagaimo/yamaimo (mountain yam) in a small bowl.
  3. Add the grated nagaimo and ¾ cup dashi (Japanese soup stock) to the bowl.
  4. Mix it all together until combined.
  5. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and let it rest in the refrigerator for at least one hour.

Steps for the Okonomiyaki Sauce

  1. Combine 1½ Tbsp sugar, 2 Tbsp oyster sauce, ¼ cup ketchup,
    and 3½ Tbsp Worcestershire sauce in a small bowl.
  2. Mix all together until the sugar is completely dissolved.

Steps for the Other Ingredient

  1. Discard the core of 1 head green cabbage and then mince the cabbage leaves.
  2. Cut ½ lb sliced pork belly in half and set aside.

Cooking Process

  1. After one hour, take out the batter from the refrigerator.
    Add 4 large eggs (50 g each w/o shell), ½ cup tenkasu/agedama (tempura scraps),
    and ¼ cup pickled red ginger (beni shoga or kizami beni shoga) to the bowl.
    Mix until combined.
  2. Add the minced cabbage to the batter, one-third of it at a time.
    Mix well before adding the rest.
  3. In a large pan, heat neutral-flavored oil on medium heat.
    When the frying pan is hot (400ºF or 200ºC), spread the batter in a circle on the pan.
    We like thicker okonomiyaki (the final thickness is ¾ inches or 2 cm).
  4. Place 2-3 slices of pork belly on top of the okonomiyaki and cook covered for 5 minutes.
  5. When the bottom side is nicely browned, flip it over.
  6. Gently press the okonomiyaki to fix its shape and keep it together.
    Cover and cook for another 5 minutes.
  7. Flip it over one last time and cook uncovered for 2 minutes.

Serving Process

  1. Serve on individual plates. Spread okonomiyaki sauce on top with a brush or spoon,
    drizzle with Japanese Kewpie mayonnaise in a zigzag pattern (optional),
    and sprinkle with katsuobushi (dried bonito flakes).You can also sprinkle with aonori
    (dried green laver seaweed) and chopped green onions/scallions and top with pickled
    red ginger (beni shoga or kizami beni shoga) for garnish.
